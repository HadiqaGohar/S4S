# Benchmark: ChatGPT vs. DeepSeek (Earth-to-Moon Distance)

This document provides a comparative analysis of responses from **ChatGPT** and **DeepSeek**. Both AI engines were tested with an identical prompt, and their outputs were verified against official **NASA** data.

---

## 1. The Prompt Used
> "What is the distance from Earth to the Moon in kilometres? Cite your reasoning."

---

## 2. Verification Against NASA Official Data

According to NASA (National Aeronautics and Space Administration):
* **Average (Mean) Distance:** **384,400 km** (238,855 miles)
* **Perigee (Closest Point):** Approx. **363,300 km**
* **Apogee (Farthest Point):** Approx. **405,500 km**

## 4. Analytical Evaluation

### A. Accuracy (Which engine was more accurate?)
* **Winner: ChatGPT**
* **Why?** Both engines correctly identified the average distance as **384,400 km**. However, when addressing the elliptical orbit variations (Perigee and Apogee), **ChatGPT provided highly accurate NASA standard values** (363,300 km and 405,500 km). 
* *DeepSeek's Error:* The range provided by DeepSeek (356k to 406k km) represents extreme astronomical gravitational perturbations rather than the standard mean perigee and apogee metrics typically found in NASA references or standard textbooks.

### B. Explanation (Which engine explained better?)
* **Winner: ChatGPT**
* **Why?** 1. **Formatting:** ChatGPT's output was incredibly clean and readable, clearly separating the "Fast answer" and "Reasoning" sections. DeepSeek’s layout was cluttered with underlying search thoughts and reasoning logs.
  2. **Perspectives:** ChatGPT introduced an extra informational dimension: *"Light takes about 1.28 seconds to travel from the Moon to Earth"*, which makes it much easier for the reader to visualize the scale.
  3. **Note:** ChatGPT's final line contained a minor structural glitch/redundancy (*"The Moon itself is Moon, and Earth is Earth."*), but its overall parameter tracking and structured layout remained superior.
